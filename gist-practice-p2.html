What is a GIST?

A GIST is a one-sentence summary that answers the most important questions about what you just read:

  • Who?
  • What?
  • When? / Where?
  • Why? / How?

🚦 Good readers know they need to "Stop and GIST." After a big paragraph, pause like you are at a Stop Sign. Ask yourself: "What was that mostly about?"

Remember the GIST rules:

  • ✅ Perfect GIST: Covers the big Who, What, and Why.
  • ❌ Too Specific: Just a small detail from the text.
  • ❌ Too Broad: Too general, missing the specific topic.
  • ❌ Incorrect: A fact that wasn't in the reading!
